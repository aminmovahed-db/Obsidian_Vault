My questions:
Q: When teams apply for pilot env, DnA give them catalog. what level of permission they need to have on the catalog and other teams cannot see their catalog? Can they create models, schemas and volumes on their own? Can teams see each others pilot catalogs?

### Answers (from [Final Solution Design](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1xHKisvagYV7-4s3_tnhAwhiHCPhIBGoVr5cthrnb9mU/edit?tab=t.0) and [Final Presentation Deck](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1Jd8ZDHcErYeLiRxl8SQoS4Gvt-GvY6kyLQd6Y6Mgqs8/edit))

**What can teams create inside their pilot catalog?**

| Asset | Can teams create? | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| Catalogs | No | DnA via Terraform only — *"the user will not have the ability to create catalogs"* |
| Schemas | Yes (option-dependent) | Teams may get `CREATE SCHEMA` — *"they may have the option to create schemas"*; Deck: *"Allows for a schema per use case / PoC"* |
| Tables/Views | Yes | *"they will have the ability to create jobs and tables"* |
| Models | Yes (implied) | DS teams develop feature tables and models in domain-specific catalogs |
| Volumes | No | DnA provisions one volume per team — *"permissions limited to READ VOLUME and WRITE VOLUME... would not have CREATE VOLUME permissions"* |

**Catch:** The Security Automation Job runs nightly to revoke `MANAGE` from creators (transfers ownership to team group), revoke ad-hoc grants to users outside the team, and grant `SELECT` to all team members. Teams are autonomous within their catalog boundary, but DnA enforces guardrails after the fact via automation.

**Can teams see each other's pilot catalogs?** No. Deck Slide 57: *"Owned by individual team — Teams X, Y or Z cannot share outside of team."* Slide 58 notes the catalog approach is better because in the old schema model *"Users can see existence of other schemas"* — catalog separation eliminates this. The only shared visibility is `pilot_uat`, where all teams get `USE CATALOG` (can see schema names but not other teams' data).

**Open question:** 📌 Whether `CREATE SCHEMA` should be team-granted or SNOW-requested is still undecided. Brad's "hard controls" stance leans toward DnA control. Good one to raise with Andrew in the workshop → [[Alinta Clarifying Questions - Allison Notes Review]]

Q: what if teams want to share some pilot information with each other?

**A:** Not needed — DnA confirmed a 2-year horizon. Solution Design: *"there is not a need for teams within the 'Pilot for Teams' environment to share datasets across teams. The only datasets that they would be accessing outside of their team data assets are datasets available within a 'Shared' schema."* The Entitlements Framework (ABAC) was deferred entirely because of this. Allison's notes flag it as a known gap (*"Cross-business access needs to be solved for"*) but not yet. The future pattern (per [[Alinta Detailed Design - Catalog and Security#2.8 Cross-Team Access (Future)]]) is: SNOW ticket → DnA grants time-bound `SELECT` on specific schema → security job auto-revokes after 90 days. **Workshop question:** What about cross-team dependencies during UAT? (Q5 in [[Alinta Clarifying Questions - Allison Notes Review]])
